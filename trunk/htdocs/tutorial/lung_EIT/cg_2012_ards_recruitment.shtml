EIT-based regional lung mechanics of a patient with the acute respiratory distress syndrome during a recruitment manouevre (human)

Methods and Data

The EIT data were collected at Children's Hospital Boston in 2009 during a stepwise lung recruitment maneuver and positive end-expiratory pressure (PEEP) titration of a patient with the acute respiratory distress syndrome as part of a clinical study described in:

    GK Wolf, C Gómez-Laberge, JN Kheir, D Zurakowski, BK Walsh, A Adler, JH Arnold. Reversal of Dependent Lung Collapse Predicts Response to Lung Recruitment in Children with Early Acute Lung Injury Pediatr Crit Care Med, In Press.
The methods described here use the raw EIT data and pressure measurements made at the airway opening to estimate the extent of regional lung overdistension and collapse during mechanical ventilation at various levels of PEEP. The methods and results are reported in:
    C Gómez-Laberge, JH Arnold, GK Wolf. A Unified Approach for EIT Imaging of Regional Overdistension and Atelectasis in Acute Lung Injury IEEE Trans Med Imag, In Press.
