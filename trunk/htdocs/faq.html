Frequently Asked Questions

  1. What is EIDORS3D?

    The goal of EIDORS3D is to promote collaboration between groups working on Electrical Impedance Tomography (EIT) and Diffusion based Optical Tomography, in medical and industrial settings.

  2. How is EIDORS3D pronounced?

    EIDORS3D is pronounced like eiderdown. That means is sounds like eye doors three dee.
    Note: correct pronounciation will make you compliant with Bill Lionheart.

  3. What is the logo?

    The logo is a walrus. EIDORS3D images blobby objects in aqueous media; similarly, the walrus is a fat, blobby animal that lives in water. The original (high resolution) image of the one used here is from the NOAA. See www.photolib.noaa.gov/animals/anim0030.htm. For another great picture of fat waruses see this image.

  4. Whom should I blame or thank or contact for more information?

    The current maintainers of EIDORS3D are Bill Lionheart and Andy Adler. Please contact them with suggestions, contribution of code, and contribution of sample data, meshes, and algorithms.

  5. Under what terms is EIDORS3D licenced

    EIDORS3D is licenced under the GNU General Public License. Uses are free to use, modify, and distribute their modifications. All modifications must include the source code, or instructions on how to obtain it.

  6. May I include EIDORS3D in a commercial product

    Yes. As long as you make the source code for EIDORS and all possible modifications to it available.

  7. How should I refer to EIDORS3D in a scientific publication

    For the core EIDORS3D code, based on the work of Nick Polydorides, you may quote the following paper

    Nick Polydorides and William R B Lionheart 2002 A Matlab toolkit for three-dimensional electrical impedance tomography: a contribution to the Electrical Impedance and Diffuse Optical Reconstruction Software project Meas. Sci. Technol. 13(12):1871-1883
    Code which implements other published algorithms will also be included with EIDORS3D. References to the appropriate publications will be listed in the source code. Hopefully, we will include a list of references here, also.

  8. Does EIDORS3D support 2D algorithms

    Yes.

  9. What if I notice a bug?

    You may write an email to the maintainers.

  10. I need some features that are missing in EIDORS3D?

    Please note that EIDORS3D is a volunteer project by some very busy people. You are welcome to make suggestions for new features, but we are unlikely to be able to find the time to implement them.

    Good options are: 1) implement new features yourself, and contribute them to EIDORS3D, or 2) find a source of funds to pay for the features you need. In either case, you are welcome to contact the maintainers to discuss.
